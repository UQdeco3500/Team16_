Barbell Curls

1. Bench press: mainly exercises the thickness of pectoralis major muscles and chest groove Action: Two hand dumbbells lying on the bench, dumbbells on the shoulders, palm facing up, push up the dumbbells until the arms are straight, pause, and then slowly restore. Tip: Push up and fall in an arc, so that the pectoralis major muscle is fully contracted and thoroughly extended.

2. Upward oblique press: mainly exercises the upper chest muscle Action: The action principle is the same as the horizontal bench press, the difference is that the bench surface is adjusted to 30 to 40 degrees of inclination, lying on the top to do.

3. Flat bird: mainly practice chest middle ditch Action: On the supine bench, two hand dumbbells, palm opposite, two arms naturally straight above the chest, two arms slightly bend the elbow to both sides of the curved dumbbells to the lowest point, the chest muscle fully extended, chest muscle contractions to lift the two arms curved back.

4. Lie on your back and pull up straight arms: the best action to expand the chest and train the pectoralis major muscle and serratus anterior muscle Action: Lie on the horizontal bench with your shoulders and feet on the ground. Hold one end of the dumbbell above the chest with both hands. Slowly place the dumbbell behind the head (down) with the shoulders as the axis (feel the chest muscle and chest rib stretch), and then pull the dumbbell back to the limit.
